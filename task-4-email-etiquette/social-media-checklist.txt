The Do's: Building a Positive Presence
Audit Your Privacy Settings: Treat this like a "digital house cleaning" once a semester. Ensure your personal photos are visible only to friends and that your "Tag Review" is turned on.

Build a Professional Network: Use platforms like LinkedIn to follow companies you admire and connect with alumni from your college. It’s never too early to start networking.

Showcase Your Projects: Whether it’s a coding project, a club event you organized, or a volunteer activity, sharing your accomplishments builds a strong personal brand.

Think Before You Click: Verify news stories or controversial posts before sharing them. Being a source of reliable information makes you look mature and thoughtful.

Practice Digital Empathy: Use your platform to support peers and engage in constructive dialogue. A reputation for being kind online carries over into real-life campus opportunities.

The Don'ts: Avoiding Common Pitfalls
Don’t Post While Emotional: If you’re angry at a professor or upset about a grade, stay offline. Posts made in the heat of the moment are often the ones people regret most.

Don’t Share Sensitive Data: Never post photos of your student ID, your specific class schedule, or your exact dorm location. Privacy is a massive part of personal safety.

Don’t Ignore Academic Integrity: Avoid posting photos of exam questions or sharing links to "study sites" that could be interpreted as facilitating cheating by your university.

Don’t Trash-Talk Institutions: Avoid publicly venting about your college or previous employers. Future recruiters often look for "cultural fit" and may see this as a red flag.

Don’t Over-Share Your Location: Avoid "live-posting" your exact location when you are alone. It is much safer to post your photos after you have already left the venue.
