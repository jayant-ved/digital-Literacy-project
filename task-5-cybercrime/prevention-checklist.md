The 'Stay Safe Online' ChecklistThe digital landscape for Indian students is more connected than ever, but it also comes with sophisticated risks like AI-generated phishing and UPI "collect request" scams. Use this checklist to audit your digital habits and stay one step ahead of cybercriminals.Stay Safe Online ChecklistFinancial and UPI Safety. Never Enter PIN to Receive Money: This is the most common UPI scam. Remember: you only enter your UPI PIN to send or authorize a payment, never to receive it.. Verify Collect Requests: If you receive a notification asking you to "Approve" or "Pay," read it carefully. Scammers often send these pretending you are winning a prize, but clicking "Approve" will deduct money from your account.. Check the Merchant Name After Scanning: After scanning a QR code at a tea stall or canteen, always double-check the name that pops up on your screen before hitting pay to ensure the code hasn't been tampered with.Device and App Security. Use Official App Stores Only: Never download "modded" versions of apps (like WhatsApp Gold or cracked games) from third-party websites or Telegram. These often contain Trojans that can hijack your phone.. Enable Biometric/App Lock: Don't just lock your phone; use the built-in "App Lock" feature for your UPI apps (GPay, PhonePe, Paytm) and banking apps for an extra layer of defense.. Disable Auto-Join for Public Wi-Fi: College campuses often have open Wi-Fi. Turn off "Auto-Join" and avoid doing any banking or shopping while connected to public networks; use a VPN if you must.Personal Data and Social Media. Audit Your Social Privacy: Set your profiles to "Private" and limit who can see your contact details. Scammers scrape "About" sections to craft convincing "Emergency Scams" targeting your friends or parents.. Spot AI Phishing: Be wary of urgent messages from "professors" or "friends" asking for money or sensitive info. With Generative AI, scammers can now mimic voices and writing styles perfectly. Always verify via a secondary channel (like a quick phone call).If You Are Scammed: Act Within the Golden HourReporting financial fraud within the first 60 minutes significantly increases the chances of the bank freezing the money before the scammer can withdraw it.ChannelContact InformationNational HelplineCall 1930 (Available 24/7)Official Portalcybercrime.gov.inAction StepSave screenshots of the chat, transaction IDs, and the scammer's number/UPI ID immediately.
