Case Study: Ransomware

Ransomware is a sophisticated form of malware that encrypts a victim's files or locks their entire computer system, demanding a payment (typically in cryptocurrency) to restore access. Modern variants often employ double extortion, where attackers not only lock data but also steal sensitive information, threatening to leak it publicly if the ransom is not paid.

Step-by-Step Execution:-

A typical ransomware attack follows a structured lifecycle:

1) Initial Access: Attackers gain entry through phishing emails containing malicious links, exploiting unpatched software vulnerabilities, or using stolen credentials.

2) Lateral Movement: Once inside, the malware moves across the network to identify high-value targets like servers and backups.

3) Data Exfiltration: Before encryption, attackers covertly upload sensitive company data to their own servers to use as leverage.

4) Encryption & Notification: The software encrypts files using unbreakable algorithms. A ransom note then appears on the victim's screen, providing payment instructions.

Targeted Victims:-

While anyone can be a target, cybercriminals increasingly focus on "big game" targets that cannot afford downtime. This includes healthcare providers, educational institutions, and critical infrastructure. Small businesses are also frequently targeted, as they often have weaker security defenses.

Consequences:-

The impact is often devastating. Victims face total operational paralysis, halting production or medical services for weeks. Beyond the financial loss of the ransom itself—which can reach millions—organizations suffer from massive data breaches, reputational damage, and potential bankruptcy.
